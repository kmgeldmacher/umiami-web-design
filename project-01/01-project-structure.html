Project 1: Structure

Due Date: Wednesday, February 19, 2014, 11:59 p.m.

Points: 10

Instructions

Using valid HTML and best practices, create an HTML version of your résumé.

You are welcome to be creative in how you present the résumé, but consider your information hierarchy.

What aspects would you present to the employer if you knew they may only look at your résumé for 30 seconds? What do you want to stand out? Are you better providing a paragraph describing your experience or are lists more functional?

We have provided a sample résumé, but we suggest you explore different presentations. You may even want to consult with an on-campus career counselor or advisor in forumlating your résumé.

In terms of meeting the requirements of creating a properly structured document, we will be looking for the following:

  • The document must validate using the W3C HTML Validator
  • The document content should be structured with HTML5 tags (i.e. header, navigation, section, etc.)
  • Headlines and subheads (h1-h6), should be used in proper order throughout the document
  • The document should include navigation linking to each section of your site
  • Include a minimum of one external link
  • Images, such as a professional portrait, should be saved in the proper format and a reasonable file and image size
  • Links or images should break
  • Use of any special characters should be properly encoded
  • Use of correct style, spelling and grammar
  • The document should conform to best practices specified in lecture and/or readings

One quarter of a point (0.25) will be deducted for each error.
